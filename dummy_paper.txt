Abstract
This paper introduces a novel approach to optimizing vector embeddings for Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) systems in academic contexts. We propose a sparse-dense hybrid retrieval mechanism that improves precision by 15% on the SciDocs dataset.

1. Introduction
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) has become a standard architecture for mitigating hallucinations in Large Language Models (LLMs). However, domain-specific vocabularies, such as those found in academic research, often challenge standard dense retrieval models.

2. Methodology
We utilized a dataset of 10,000 academic papers across various disciplines. Our model, HybridRAG, combines standard dense embeddings from Sentence-Transformers with BM25 sparse retrieval. We then apply a cross-encoder for re-ranking the top 50 candidates.

3. Results
The HybridRAG model achieved an MRR@10 of 0.85 on our evaluation set, outperforming the dense-only baseline (0.74). Furthermore, qualitative analysis shows a significant reduction in hallucinated citations by the generator LLM.

4. Conclusion and Future Work
Our hybrid approach demonstrates that combining lexical and semantic search is highly effective for academic RAG. Future work will explore dynamic weighting between the sparse and dense components based on query intent.

5. Limitations
The primary limitation of our study is the computational overhead of the cross-encoder re-ranking step, which adds approximately 200ms to the overall query latency.
